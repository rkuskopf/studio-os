# Slowtides — Creative Inputs

A system for discovering new music and building inspiration. Addresses the identified problem of insufficient new musical input, which leads to creative stagnation and referencing the same handful of artists.

---

## The Problem

Without a deliberate system, music discovery defaults to:
- Spotify's algorithmic recommendations (narrow, genre-stuck)
- The same handful of reference artists on repeat
- No capture mechanism — you hear something great and forget it

---

## Discovery System

### Weekly Discovery Ritual (Sunday, 30 min)

Once a week, spend 30 min actively finding new music. Not passively listening — intentionally seeking.

**Sources to rotate through:**

| Source | What you find | How |
|--------|--------------|-----|
| **Bandcamp Daily** | Independent artists, often ambient/experimental | bandcamp.com/daily |
| **Music blogs** | Curated discovery, editorial | The Quietus, Pitchfork (Experimental section), Resident Advisor |
| **SoundCloud** | Emerging artists before Spotify | Search genre tags: ambient, cinematic, slowcore |
| **YouTube rabbit holes** | Deep cuts, live sessions, documentary-style content | Follow one thread per session |
| **Artist recommendations** | "Fans also like" on Bandcamp | More reliable than Spotify's algorithm |
| **Spotify editorial playlists** | Curated by Spotify's editorial team (not algorithmic) | "Ambient Essentials", "Peaceful Piano", "Late Night Coding" |

### Capture Rule

If you hear something that affects you, **capture it immediately**:
1. Add to the **Inspiration Playlist** in Spotify (or Bandcamp wishlist)
2. Note what specifically caught you — not just "good song" but "the way the reverb tail bleeds into the next chord" or "the drum pattern that breaks on bar 7"

## Expanding the Map

Deliberately explore adjacent genres to avoid tunnel vision:

| Genre | Artists to explore | Why it's useful |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Contemporary classical** | Arvo Pärt, Sofia Gubaidulina, Jóhann Jóhannsson | Harmonic language, tension/release, orchestration |
| **Ambient techno** | Aphex Twin, Global Communication, The Orb | Rhythm in ambient, textures, synthesis |
| **Slowcore / post-rock** | Godspeed You! Black Emperor, Low, Grouper | Patience, dynamics, emotional weight |
| **Japanese ambient** | Hiroshi Yoshimura, Midori Takada | Space, minimalism, texture as melody |
| **Neo-soul / RnB production** | Frank Ocean, Steve Lacy | Warm production, unexpected chord choices |

## Offline Inputs

Not everything comes from streaming. Deliberately seek non-screen music input:

- **Live music:** Attend one live show/concert per month minimum. Melbourne has strong ambient/electronic scene.
- **Record stores:** Browse a record store monthly. Physical browsing surfaces things algorithms won't.
- **Film soundtracks:** Watch films with notable scores — pay attention to how music serves the visual without overwhelming it.
- **Field recording:** Carry phone voice memos. Capture interesting sounds (texture sources for future tracks).
