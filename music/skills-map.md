## Priority Growth Areas

Ranked by impact on Slowtides sound and catalogue quality:

### 1. Granular Synthesis — HIGH PRIORITY
**Why:** Core to the ambient/cinematic texture of Slowtides. Granular tools (Granulator II, Ableton's built-in, Alchemy's granular mode) allow the kind of evolving, organic pads and textures that define the genre.
**Action:** Dedicate one practice block/week to granular sound design. Start with Alchemy granular engine in Logic.

### 2. Mixing Intentionality — HIGH PRIORITY
**Why:** Current mixes feel muddy or flat compared to references. Need to move from "it sounds okay" to "it sounds professional."
**Action:** Study reference tracks by Nils Frahm, Jon Hopkins, Max Richter. Do frequency analysis. Apply one new mixing technique per session.

### 3. Piano / Keys Technique — HIGH PRIORITY
**Why:** Live keys are central to the Slowtides sound. Better technique = more expressive performances = less editing needed.
**Action:** Daily 20-min keys practice. Focus on left-hand independence, chord voicings, and slow melodic lines.

### 4. Music Theory — Harmonic Tension — MEDIUM PRIORITY
**Why:** Understanding how to build and release tension is the difference between memorable and forgettable.
**Action:** Study one new harmonic concept per week (suspensions, secondary dominants, borrowed chords).

### 5. Sampling / Manipulation — MEDIUM PRIORITY
**Why:** Textural samples (found sounds, processed field recordings) add depth and uniqueness.
**Action:** Explore Logic's Sampler and third-party tools. Experiment with one sampled texture per new track.

### 6. Mastering Chain — MEDIUM PRIORITY (learn or outsource)
**Why:** Releasing music without proper mastering sounds amateur.
**Action decision needed:** Learn Logic's mastering tools + Ozone basics, OR budget for outsourced mastering per release.
