## WHAT IS THE PROBLEM WITH THE GOODS? (Use this exact text)

```
1. I purchased a MacBook Pro 15-inch (2018 model), Serial Number C02YC5T3JG5H, from Apple Australia on 29 March 2019 for $3,500.

2. From early in its life, the MacBook exhibited recurring faults including speaker crackling, battery degradation, and keyboard issues.

3. On 28 January 2025, the MacBook suffered a major power failure. I took it to an authorised service provider (My Byte, Service Order 20033891) on 29 January 2025.

4. My Byte diagnosed a logic board and Touch ID failure. On professional advice, I paid $298 for data migration and restore on 4 February 2025, as attempting repair would risk data loss. The underlying logic board fault remained unresolved.

5. In late November 2025, less than 10 months after the first major failure, the MacBook suffered a total logic board failure and would not power on.

6. I contacted Apple on 26 November 2025 and opened an ACL claim (Case 102760914137).

7. On 2 December 2025, Apple Highpoint assessed the device (Case 102765223980, Work Authorization R693726699). Apple confirmed: (a) logic board and Touch ID failure; (b) parts unavailable; (c) device cannot be repaired.

8. Apple's Work Authorization answered "Yes" to: "Is it within a reasonable period from purchase?"

9. On 4 December 2025, my case was transferred to Senior Advisor Ryan. During a recorded phone call, Ryan acknowledged that the speaker crackling and keyboard faults I had reported were "more than likely" consistent with an underlying logic board issue. Ryan confirmed I had previously reported battery degradation to Apple.

10. Ryan committed to reviewing Apple's records of my earlier fault reports. Ryan never followed up.

11. On 10 December 2025, Apple rejected my ACL claim despite their Work Authorization confirming the failure occurred within a reasonable period.

12. On 6 January 2026, I submitted a detailed reassessment request with supporting documentation.

13. On 12 January 2026, Ryan called and advised me not to proceed with the reassessment. Ryan promised to investigate Apple Store records of my earlier fault reports. Ryan never followed up.

14. On 3 February 2026, I sent a formal complaint to Apple with a 10-day deadline (17 February 2026).

15. Apple did not respond to my formal complaint by the stated deadline.

16. The MacBook Pro failed twice within 10 months due to the same underlying logic board defect. The device is unrepairable and unusable. Apple's own assessment confirmed the failure occurred within a reasonable period from purchase. A premium device costing $3,500 should last significantly longer than 5-6 years with light use. Apple has breached the consumer guarantees under the Australian Consumer Law.
```
