# Outreach Templates

Two modes:
- **Cold outreach** — they don't know you. Be warm, specific, human. Show you've actually looked at their work.
- **Warm outreach** — you have a connection (referral, met briefly, online interaction). Be direct, get to the point.

---

## Cold Outreach — Warm & Specific

Use when: reaching out to someone who has no prior relationship with you.

**Goal:** Get a reply. Not to sell — just to open a conversation.

**Rules:**
- Reference something real and specific about their brand/product
- One observation, one question or offer — that's it
- Keep it short. If it looks like work to read, it won't get read.
- Do not mention rates, packages, or "full-service solutions"
- Send from studio@kspf.au with a kspf.au signature

---

### Template C1 — Brand observation (Instagram/email)

> Subject: Your brand — a thought
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I've been following [Brand] for a while — the [specific thing: packaging / visual direction / tone of voice] is genuinely interesting. I can see where you're going with it.
>
> I'm Rowan — I run KSPF, a small creative studio in Melbourne. I work with early-stage brands on identity, packaging, and the visual side of building something from scratch.
>
> I had a few thoughts about [Brand] that I think could be useful. Would you be open to a short conversation?
>
> Rowan
> kspf.au

**Personalise:** The bracketed sections are mandatory — don't send without filling them in specifically.

## Warm Outreach — Direct & Brief

Use when: past client, referral you've already spoken to, someone you've had an online exchange with, or an existing contact you're reactivating.

**Goal:** Get a meeting or a "yes, let's talk." They already know you or have a reason to trust you.

**Rules:**
- No setup needed — get to the point in the first sentence
- Be specific about what you want (a call, a catch-up, a reply)
- One clear ask per message

---

### Template W1 — Past client check-in

> Subject: Checking in
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Hope things are going well. I've been heads-down with a few projects since we wrapped [project name] — wanted to check in and see how things are going on your end.
>
> If there's ever anything coming up that I could help with, or anyone in your network I should know — I'd love to hear about it.
>
> Rowan

---

### Template W2 — Referral ask (after delivering Alt Cosmetics)

> Hi Anh,
>
> Now that we're wrapping up — I just wanted to say this has been a great project to work on. The brand is looking genuinely strong.
>
> I'm starting to grow the studio a bit, and I'm looking for my next one or two clients. If anyone in your network — salon clients, suppliers, anyone building a product or brand — comes to mind, I'd really appreciate an intro.
>
> No pressure at all. Just wanted to ask.
>
> Rowan

---

### Template W3 — Reactivating a cold lead who replied but went quiet

> Hi [Name],
>
> Following up from our conversation a while back. Are you still working on [what they mentioned]?
>
> Happy to pick up where we left off if the timing is better now.
>
> Rowan

---

## Follow-Up Rules

- Wait **5–7 business days** before following up on cold outreach
- One follow-up only on cold leads — if no reply after two messages, mark as Lost/Paused and move on
- Warm leads: follow up sooner (2–3 days), more directly
- Track all send dates in the pipeline (`leads/_pipeline.md`) and Asana
