# ALT. Cosmetics — Letterform References: The Letter A

*How to find strong, historically-grounded "A" letterforms suitable for a logo mark.*

---

## What you're looking for

An "A" with enough character to stand alone as a mark — not a font glyph, but something that reads as designed: architectural, carved, pressed, or drawn with intent. The best sources are objects that were made to last: coins, seals, crests, inscriptions, and monogram sheets.

---

## Primary Research Sources

### Numismatics (Ancient & Medieval Coins)
Old coins have some of the most resolved letterforms ever made — every stroke had to survive at 15mm diameter.

| Source | What's there | Link |
|--------|-------------|------|

### Seals, Intaglios & Gems (Direction A territory)

## Key Books (available free on Internet Archive)

| Title | Why useful |
|-------|-----------|
| *Alphabets and Ornaments* — Ernst Lehner | Classic compendium of historic alphabets from 5,000 years — includes carved, inscribed, calligraphic, and architectural forms |
| *Lettering: Its History and Application* | Covers Roman inscriptions through Art Nouveau |
| *The Book of Monograms* (various Victorian editions) | Hundreds of interlocking letterform monograms — search `"book of monograms"` on archive.org |
| *Art Alphabets and Lettering* — J.M. Bergling | Art nouveau and deco alphabets, high resolution |
| *The Art of Illuminating* — W.J. Loftie | Medieval illuminated letterforms including A |

## Recommended workflow

1. **Pinterest boards first** — run all the search terms above and bulk-save into a dedicated board (`ALT. — A letterform references`). Use Pinloader Chrome extension to bulk-download later.
2. **Internet Archive for depth** — download 2–3 of the books above as PDFs, skim for A forms worth extracting.
3. **Museum collections for clean scans** — The Met and British Museum both offer free high-res downloads. Search `seal`, `coin`, `inscription` and filter by Greek/Roman/Medieval period.
4. **Screenshot the strongest 10–15** across directions — pin a "shortlist" board or folder before moving to Illustrator.
