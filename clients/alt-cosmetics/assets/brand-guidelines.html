Brand Overview

Beautiful because it works. Designed because it deserves to be.

About ALT.

ALT. Cosmetics is a design-forward premium beauty brand launching with a single hero product: a plant-based, peptide-rich lash serum. Founded by Anh Tran — experienced lash technician and cosmetic tattoo artist.

The brand sits between overtly clinical (Clinique, pharmacy-adjacent) and mass-lifestyle (Grande, Zali). It neither shouts nor explains itself. It earns its place through restraint, finish, and intention.

Positioning

ALT. occupies whitespace in the Australian beauty market: design-forward aesthetics paired with genuine clinical credibility. Not pharmacy-cold, not lifestyle-loud. Quietly confident.

Brand Character

Quietly confident. Doesn't explain itself. Thrives in simplicity. Not afraid of appearing shallow. Doesn't follow trends, doesn't deliberately subvert them either. Aesthetics as an extension of sense and pleasure.

Material Context

Chrome is the brand's defining material — but it requires balancing. In photography and environmental applications, pair chrome with:

Organic textures

Linen, raw cotton, natural fibres, dried botanicals, stone surfaces, raw plaster, soft shadows

Warm tones

Cream, sand, blush, terracotta undertones. Avoid cold greys or blue-whites.

This balance ensures the brand remains approachable and liveable — industrial enough to command attention, warm enough to feel welcome.

Tone of Voice

Precise. Confident. Uncluttered. ALT. states rather than suggests. Warm in the way a trusted expert is warm — not effusive.

Voice Principles

Confident

We know our product works. We don't hedge or over-qualify. Statements are direct and assured.

Precise

Specificity over vagueness. We say what we mean. Every word earns its place.

Restrained

No exclamation marks. No hyperbole. We don't shout — our quality speaks for itself.

Expert

Founded by a professional. We speak with authority because we have genuine knowledge.

Language to Avoid

Certain words and phrases undermine the brand's positioning. Avoid wellness clichés and beauty industry hyperbole:

Avoid these words

ritual · glow · nourishing · luxurious · indulgent · pamper · transform · miracle · revolutionary · game-changer · obsessed · literally · amazing · clean beauty · self-care · wellness · journey

Avoid these patterns

Exclamation marks · ALL CAPS for emphasis · Questions as headlines · Over-promising ("lashes you've always dreamed of") · Diminutives ("little" "just" "simply")

Alternatives

Instead of

"Nourishing formula for gorgeous lashes!"

Write

"Peptide-rich formula supports lash condition."

Instead of

"Transform your lash game"

Write

"Designed for visible results."

19

Messaging Hierarchy

When space is limited, prioritise messaging in this order:

1. Brand name — ALT. or ALT. COSMETICS
2. Product descriptor — Lash Serum
3. Key benefit — Plant-based peptide formula
4. Proof point — Results in 4–6 weeks
5. Supporting details — Prostaglandin-free, Australian made

Note: "Prostaglandin-free" and "clean" are table stakes in 2026 — not differentiators. Don't lead with them.

Photography

Editorial, considered, high-contrast. Photography should feel intentional — every element placed with purpose.

Photography guidelines and example imagery to be developed. Key references: Alaïa, Prada, retaW — high-contrast, editorial, considered.

Photography Principles

Composition

Clean, uncluttered frames. Generous negative space. Product as focal point, not competing with props.

Lighting

Studio lighting preferred. Soft shadows add dimension. Avoid flat, even lighting or harsh direct light.

Material pairing

Chrome product against warm, organic textures. Linen, stone, dried botanicals, natural fibres.

Colour treatment

Muted, desaturated tones. Warm neutrals. No heavy filters or colour casts.

21

Photography Don'ts

Don't

Use lifestyle imagery with excessive props

Don't

Show product in use on models (for now)

Don't

Use cool-toned or blue-white backgrounds

Don't

Over-retouch or add artificial shine
