## 1. Brand Foundation

### Brand Essence

ALT. exists at the intersection of clinical precision and design intention. Products are formulated to solve real concerns, presented as objects worthy of display.

### Positioning

| Axis | Position |
|------|----------|
| Ritual/Lifestyle ←→ Clinical/Efficacy | Center-right (slightly clinical) |
| Bold/Editorial ←→ Quiet/Restrained | Upper (editorial, bold) |

**Reference territory:** Acne Studios, Nolew, Prada Beauty  
**Avoid:** Aesop (too ritual), GrandeLash (too mass), The Ordinary (too budget)

### Brand Personality

| Spectrum | Position |
|----------|----------|
| Elite ←→ Mass Appeal | Slightly Elite |
| Serious ←→ Playful | Center (balanced) |
| Conventional ←→ Rebel | Slightly Rebel |
| Friend ←→ Authority | Slightly Authority |
| Mature & Classic ←→ Young & Innovative | Center-right (leaning innovative) |

### The Contrast Principle

ALT. lives in **intentional tension**. The mark and brand system pull from different places:

| Element | Character |
|---------|-----------|
| **Mark** | Organic, flowing, abstract, hand-drawn feeling |
| **Wordmark** | Geometric, hard, extended, mechanical |
| **Packaging** | Chrome, clinical, precision-engineered |
| **Experience** | Object for the home, considered, special |

They shouldn't look like they came from the same place — that's deliberate.

## 2. Logo Architecture

### 2.1 Primary Elements

| Element | Description | Primary Use |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Wordmark** | ALT. set in Aktiv Grotesk Extended | Hero identification, packaging front |
| **Mark** | Abstract monogram (TBD) | Discovery element, closures, small scale |
| **Lockup** | Wordmark + mark combined | Limited use — brand collateral |

### 2.2 Logo Architecture Chart

| Application | Asset | Minimum Size |
|-------------|-------|--------------|
| **Hero products** (feature packaging) | Wordmark | 15mm cap height |
| **Core range** (standard packaging) | Wordmark | 10mm cap height |
| **Closures & caps** | Mark only | 5mm |
| **Base stamps** | Mark only | 3mm |
| **Digital — desktop** | Wordmark or lockup | 80px cap height |
| **Digital — mobile** | Mark only (nav) | 24px |
| **Favicon** | Mark only | 16×16px |

### 2.3 Clear Space

Minimum clear space = **cap height of the "A"** on all sides.

The cap height of "A" serves as the **base unit (1×)** for all spatial relationships in the brand system.

```
        ┌─────────────────────────┐
        │         1×              │
        │    ┌───────────┐        │
        │ 1× │   ALT.    │ 1×     │
        │    └───────────┘        │
        │         1×              │
        └─────────────────────────┘
```

### 2.4 Small-Scale Optimisation

When cap height falls below **5mm (print) or 14pt (digital)**:

| Adjustment | Rationale |
|------------|-----------|
| Increase letter-spacing by +20 | Prevents letters from optically closing |
| Consider mark-only | Wordmark may lose legibility |
| Avoid reversed (white on dark) | Chrome reflection compounds legibility issues |

**Debossing / hot-stamp note:** Test at final production scale. Chrome surfaces require deeper deboss for legibility.

### 2.5 Incorrect Usage

- ❌ Do not stretch or distort
- ❌ Do not change typeface
- ❌ Do not add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)
- ❌ Do not rotate
- ❌ Do not place on busy photography without sufficient contrast
- ❌ Do not use low-resolution assets

## 3. Typography

### 3.1 Type System Overview

| Role | Typeface | Weight | Use |
|------|----------|--------|-----|
| **Primary** | Aktiv Grotesk Extended | Medium–Bold | Headlines, wordmark, hero text |
| **Secondary** | ⚠️ TBD — NOT mono | Regular, Italic | Body copy, functional text |
| **Tertiary** | Aktiv Grotesk (standard width) | Light–Regular | Legal, ingredients, fine print |

> **Note:** Secondary typeface under consideration. Direction: refined serif or humanist sans that adds warmth without compromising clinical tone. Avoid: mono/typewriter (rejected by client).

### 3.2 Type Hierarchy

| Level | Typeface | Size (Desktop) | Size (Mobile) | Weight | Case |
|-------|----------|----------------|---------------|--------|------|
| **H1 — Hero** | Aktiv Extended | 48–72pt | 32–40pt | Bold | ALL CAPS |
| **H2 — Section** | Aktiv Extended | 32–40pt | 24–28pt | Medium | ALL CAPS |
| **H3 — Subsection** | Aktiv Extended | 20–24pt | 18–20pt | Medium | Title Case |
| **Body** | Secondary TBD | 16–18pt | 14–16pt | Regular | Sentence case |
| **Caption** | Secondary TBD | 12–14pt | 11–12pt | Regular | Sentence case |
| **Legal/Ingredients** | Aktiv Grotesk | 8–10pt | 7–9pt | Light | Sentence case |

### 3.3 Type Setting Rules

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Leading (line height)** | 1.2× (headlines), 1.5× (body) | Tighter for impact, looser for readability |
| **Tracking — Headlines** | +20 to +50 | Extended faces need room to breathe |
| **Tracking — Body** | 0 to +10 | Minimal adjustment |
| **Tracking — Small scale** | +20 to +40 | Compensate for optical closing |
| **Alignment** | Left-aligned (primary), Centered (hero moments) | Avoid justified |
| **Line length** | 45–75 characters | Optimal readability for body copy |

### 3.4 Rags and Widows

- Avoid orphans (single words on final line)
- Avoid widows (single lines at top of column)
- Manual line breaks preferred over auto-hyphenation
- Headlines should break at natural phrase points

### 3.5 Ingredients & Functional Copy

For regulatory and ingredient text:

| Element | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|
| **INCI list** | Aktiv Grotesk Light, 7–8pt, sentence case |
| **Batch/expiry** | Aktiv Grotesk Light, 6–7pt |
| **Warnings** | Aktiv Grotesk Regular, 8pt minimum |
| **Usage instructions** | Secondary typeface, 10–12pt |

Maintain generous leading (1.6×) for dense functional text to preserve clinical clarity.

- **Photography tints** — Stone at 10–20% overlay for cohesion
- **No brights** — accent colours not currently in system

## 5. Composition & Grids

### 5.1 The Base Unit

All spacing derives from the **cap height of "A" in the wordmark** (referred to as **1×**).

| Multiple | Use |
|----------|-----|
| 0.5× | Minimum internal padding |
| 1× | Standard element spacing, margins |
| 2× | Section breaks |
| 4× | Major divisions, page margins |

### 5.2 Grid Systems

#### Print — A4 Portrait (210 × 297mm)

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Columns | 12 |
| Gutter | 4mm |
| Margins | 15mm (all sides) |
| Baseline grid | 4mm |

#### Print — A5 / Postcard

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Columns | 6 |
| Gutter | 3mm |
| Margins | 10mm |

#### Digital — Desktop (1440px)

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Columns | 12 |
| Gutter | 24px |
| Margins | 80px |
| Max content width | 1200px |

#### Digital — Mobile (375px)

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Columns | 4 |
| Gutter | 16px |
| Margins | 20px |

### 5.3 Fine Lines

Use 0.25–0.5pt rules (Ink at 40% opacity) to:
- Subdivide layouts
- Separate content blocks
- Create visual rhythm

Fine lines reinforce the clinical, graphic sensibility without adding visual weight.

### 5.4 White Space

White space is a feature, not a limitation. Compositions should feel:
- Considered, not crowded
- Confident in restraint
- Premium through absence

**Minimum white space:** 40% of any composition should be empty.

## 6. Photography & Imagery

### 6.1 Photography Style

| Attribute | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Lighting** | Clean, diffused, studio-controlled |
| **Colour** | Desaturated, warm-neutral, no heavy colour casts |
| **Composition** | Graphic, considered negative space |
| **Focus** | Sharp product focus, shallow depth acceptable for detail shots |
| **Styling** | Minimal, architectural surfaces (concrete, stone, linen) |

### 6.2 Post-Processing Rules

| ✅ Allowed | ❌ Forbidden |
|-----------|-------------|
| Subtle exposure/contrast adjustment | Heavy filters or presets |
| Gentle highlight recovery | Unnatural colour grading |
| Minor skin retouching (lifestyle) | Over-smoothing or "plastic" skin |
| Dust/scratch removal | Adding lens flares or overlays |
| Desaturation for cohesion | Oversaturation |

### 6.3 Chrome in Photography

Reflective surfaces require special handling:

- Shoot chrome at angles that show material quality without blowing highlights
- Use gradient backgrounds (Linen → Bone) to create soft reflections
- Logo placement: position where reflection doesn't obscure
- Post-production: recover chrome detail, don't clip to pure white

### 6.4 Material Pairing

Show chrome against complementary materials:

| Pairing | Mood |
|---------|------|
| Chrome + concrete | Industrial, gallery |
| Chrome + linen | Soft, domestic |
| Chrome + stone | Premium, architectural |
| Chrome + skin | Intimate, product-in-use |

**Avoid:** Chrome on chrome, chrome on pure white (loses definition), chrome on saturated colours.

## 7. Materiality & Finishes

### 7.1 Signature Finish — Chrome

| Property | Specification |
|----------|---------------|
| **Finish** | High-polish reflective chrome |
| **Application** | Vacuum metallisation or plating |
| **Logo application** | Deboss, hot-stamp, or laser etch |
| **Touch** | Smooth, cool, substantial weight |

Chrome is the **hero material**. It should feel engineered, not decorative.

### 7.2 Secondary Materials

| Material | Use | Notes |
|----------|-----|-------|
| **Matte white** | Secondary packaging, inserts | Creates contrast with chrome |
| **Uncoated paper** | Collateral, cards | Warm, tactile, premium feel |
| **Soft-touch coating** | Box exteriors | Adds intimacy to unboxing |
| **Clear (limited)** | Windows only if essential | Avoid clear plastic tubes |

### 7.3 Print Finishes

| Finish | Use |
|--------|-----|
| **Blind deboss** | Wordmark on uncoated paper |
| **Foil (silver)** | Accent only — use sparingly |
| **Spot UV** | Mark only — subtle discovery moment |

## 8. Functional Elements

### 8.1 Functional Icon System

⚠️ **Status:** Not yet developed

**Direction:** Simple, geometric, single-weight line icons for:
- Usage instructions (apply, wait, rinse)
- Product benefits (peptide, vegan, sensitivity-tested)
- Shelf life / PAO symbols
- Recycling information

**Style notes:**
- Match Aktiv Grotesk geometric construction
- Single stroke weight (1.5pt at standard size)
- Ink colour only
- Optical size adjustments for small scale

### 8.2 Infographics

If clinical data or efficacy claims require visualisation:

| Element | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|
| **Data points** | Ink (solid) |
| **Axes and grids** | Stone at 50% |
| **Backgrounds** | Linen or Bone |

**Avoid:** Pharmacy-cold aesthetics (pure white backgrounds, blue accents).

|------|-----------|------|

| **Recycling symbols** | Base | 4mm minimum |
| **Batch code** | Base | 6pt minimum |
| **Country of origin** | Back panel | 8pt minimum |

## 9. Tone of Voice

### 9.1 Voice Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Precise** | Every word earns its place. No filler. |
| **Assured** | States, doesn't sell. Confidence without arrogance. |
| **Uncluttered** | Short sentences. Clear structure. |
| **Warm expert** | Knowledgeable but approachable. Not cold. |

### 9.2 Dos and Don'ts

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|------|---------|
| "Formulated with biotin peptides" | "Infused with the nourishing power of..." |
| "Apply nightly for 8 weeks" | "Make it part of your self-care ritual!" |
| "Clinically tested" | "Revolutionary breakthrough formula" |
| "Made in Australia" | "Proudly Australian-made with love" |

### 9.3 Avoid These Words

| Category | Banned Words |
|----------|--------------|
| **Wellness clichés** | ritual, glow, nourishing, radiant, luminous |
| **Hype** | revolutionary, breakthrough, miracle, amazing |
| **Gendered** | girly, pamper, indulge |
| **Exclamation marks** | ! (never use) |

### 9.4 Headlines

- ALL CAPS in Aktiv Extended
- Short (2–5 words ideal)
- No punctuation unless essential
- State the benefit or name, nothing more

**Examples:**
- LASH SERUM
- 8 WEEKS. VISIBLE RESULTS.
- PEPTIDE FORMULA
