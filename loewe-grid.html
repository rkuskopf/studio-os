Circle system
Why single circles don't work: Each stroke uses two circles in sequence. The main body follows a large primary arc (r ≈ 92px), then the path transitions — without breaking — to a smaller secondary circle (r ≈ 35px ≈ r/φ²) for the terminal hook. This transition is smooth because the two circles are internally tangent: they share exactly one point, (223, 223) for the top-left form. At that point, the curvature flips but the direction stays continuous.

The secondary circles are not at the quadrant center — they sit 57px toward the logo's center along the diagonal, which is why a simple 4-circle inscribed grid never explains the hooks. Toggle both circle layers together to see the full system.
